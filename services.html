Treatments

Everything we treat, explained plainly.

No jargon-first explanations. Each treatment below covers what it's for, roughly how long it takes, and what to expect in the chair.

Checkups Whitening Fillings & Crowns Implants Clear Aligners Children's Dentistry
01

Checkups & Cleaning

A full exam, professional cleaning, and a digital scan to catch problems while they're still small and inexpensive to fix. We recommend a check every six months — more often if you're prone to gum sensitivity.

  • Duration: 30–45 minutes
  • Frequency: Every 6 months
  • Numbing needed: No
02

Whitening

In-chair whitening lifts shade in a single session using a calibrated gel and light; take-home trays work more gradually if you prefer a lower-intensity option. We check your enamel and existing fillings first, since whitening only works on natural tooth surface.

  • Duration: 60–90 minutes (in-chair)
  • Results last: 6–12 months typically
  • Numbing needed: No
03

Fillings & Crowns

Cavities are filled with tooth-colored composite rather than metal amalgam. For more extensive damage, we design and mill ceramic crowns on-site with CEREC technology — meaning most crowns are finished in one visit instead of two.

  • Duration: 30 min (filling) · ~2 hrs (same-day crown)
  • Numbing needed: Yes, with topical numbing first
  • Follow-up: Usually none required
04

Implants

A titanium post replaces the root of a missing tooth, topped with a ceramic crown that matches your bite. We plan placement using 3D imaging beforehand so the fit is precise and healing is predictable.

  • Duration: Placement ~1 hr; healing 2–4 months
  • Numbing needed: Yes, local anesthesia
  • Lifespan: 15+ years with normal care
05

Clear Aligners

A series of custom, near-invisible trays gradually shift teeth into place. You'll get a full digital preview of your projected result before committing, and swap to a new tray roughly every two weeks.

  • Duration: Typically 4–9 months total
  • Check-ins: Every 6–8 weeks
  • Numbing needed: No
06

Children's Dentistry

First visits are kept short and low-pressure — usually just a look, a count, and a chance to get comfortable in the chair. We talk through any treatment in language a child understands before doing anything.

  • First visit age: Around age 2–3, or first tooth
  • Duration: 15–20 minutes
  • Parent present: Always welcome
